    • Panda

  • Pandas have an extended wrist bone that acts like a thumb to help them grab food
  • Pandas eat a lot of bamboo-from 26 to 84 pounds of it a day
  • Pandas's fur helps them camoflauge with its environment

    • Crab

  • Softshell crabs are simply crabs that have recently molted, so any crab can be a softshell crab
  • Hermit crabs, king Crabs, and horseshoe crabs are false crabs.
  • A group of crabs is called a cast.

    • Duck

  • Ducks are Omnivores
  • Ducks lay more eggs if there is more daylight
  • If properly cared for, ducks can live up to 20 years old.

    • Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

    • Chameleon

  • Chameleons can change color to camoflauge with their environment.
  • Chameleons' toungues can be twice as long as their bodies.
  • Chameleons are vertebrates.

    • Pigeon

  • Pigeons feed their young with a special fluid called "Pigeon milk."
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons are very clean animals and they are not significant disease transmitters.

    • Flying Cicada

  • Flying Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Females Flying Cicadas may be attracted to the sound of motors.
